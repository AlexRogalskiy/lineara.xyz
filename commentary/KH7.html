This is one of only two opisthographic tablets from Khania (cf. KH 97).
.a: The repetition of the pair VIR+*313b and *303+D looks like it might reflect a fixed ratio: J : 10 as B : 4 as 1JEL 2 : 18. Taking the first one, J:10, that ratio = 1/2 : 10 or 1:20. If the ratio is the same for the other citations, then B is to 4 as 1 is to 20, and thus B = 1/5 (cf. KH 4), not 1/3. If the same ratio holds for 1JEL 2 , we would have to correct 18 to 38 (18 is certain, but there is room for two more ten-dashes): thus, 1JE + L 2 = 38/20, or 7/4 + L 2 = 38/20, or 35/20 +L 2 = 38/20, or L 2 = 3/20.
a.3: I-JA-PA-ME may be a religious heading; cf. I-JA-PA[ (IO Za 2), I-NA-JA-PA-QA (Libation Formula word 8).
