I suggest this tablet represents the work of two different scribes. The first on HT95a recording a state of allocation of grain to six different entities, while on HT95b the second scribe records an update to this state after some elapse of time.

The first point of interest is how rounds the numbers all are. Each entry has ten against it except for two: QE-RA2-U with 7 and SA-RU with 20. This suggests a broad-brush assesment of some sort, for example how much of something each entity owes or is owed. The ‘something’ in this seems to be quantities of grain ( 𐙉), the logogram after DA-DU-MA-TA. There aren’t any clues as to what DA-DU-MA-TA is but A-DU at the start of HT95B is thought to indicate something like ‘assessment’. And indeed there seems to be a relationship between SARU given a quantity of 10 in relation to ‘ADU’ on the reverse side where it is the only one on the obverse side to have a value of 20:

It seems logical to infer that ADU SARA 10 is somehow referring back to the fact that while everyone else in the previous list has been assigned 10 only SARU is assigned 20. On top of this it also seems relevant that after ADU SARA 10 all the others are listed again assigned a value of 10, as though they are the only ones who need to ‘top up’. What would support this interpretation is if we could make out what occurs between ‘SARU 10’ and ‘DAME 10’.

Unfortunately the logogram there is obscured by a break in the tablet:

There isn’t currently a good guess to what this logogram might be and it may well be just a word separator. It’s tempting to look at it as ‘MA’ (𐙁), as it does look similar to the ‘MA’  in DA-DU-MA-TA on the other side of the tablet:

Whatever the identity of this effaced character it must record some operation on the listing that follows it, similar to A-DU.

The relationship between the listing of DA-ME, MI-NU-TE, SA-RU, KU-NI-SU, DI-DE-RU and QE-RA2-U  on HT95a and HT95b is obvious and HT95b is certainly recording some alteration to the facts recorded on HT95a (or the other way around). The changes are:

  • SA-RU has had its allocation of 20 altered to 10 by an operation involving A-DU
  • The amount associated with QE-RA2-U has been altered from 7 to 10.

This alteration to QE-RA2-U is clouded by the damage to that part of the tablet. The reading of 10 is not certain, but it is unlikely to be 7. Displaying the tablets side by side shows as much:

A different scribe for each side would make HT95b an update to the state recorded in HT95a that has occurred after some elapse in time by a co-worker of the original scribe. The damage to the end of HT95b allows the possibilty that it does in fact record 7 for QE-RA2-U. It would certainly seem strange to record an update to QE-RA2-U without accounting for it by an operation such as A-DU.
