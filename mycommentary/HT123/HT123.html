  • The ratio between the values given for 𐙋 and 𐙜 for each district and in total is roughly 3 to 1.
  • The ‘short-fall’ amount given in each case relates to 𐙜.
  • Adding the short-fall amount to 𐙜 brings the ratio with 𐙋 more exact for each district (and for the total) but it is still only approximately 3 to 1 except in the case of DA-TU where it is exact.
  • The ‘short-fall’ amounts for each district don’t add up to the total short-fall amount, so there is clearly an expectation for the total yield of 𐙜 in relation to 𐙋 that is independent of the individual shortfall from each district. In other words, an expected total yield such as was achieved in a previous year or round of contribution.

The nature of the yield product (𐙜)  is almost certainly not olive oil as this has its own logogram and a range of ligatures that presumably denote different types of oil. I suggest the reverse side of the tablet (HT123b) deals with the distribution of the yield product. This is motivated by observing that the total of 20 and short-fall of 5[.35] given at the end of the HT123b equals the total yield of 25.35 given for 𐙜 on HT123a (this is assuming that the fractional amount of .35 for the shortfall is effaced from HT123b due to the bottom left hand corner being missing).

The fact that an allocation of 1 unit of 𐙢 to ‘𐙓’ is listed separately from its main allocation of 11 𐙜 does offer some support, but if we assume that the units in this section relate to 𐙢 anyway that by itself would be reason enough for a separate listing for  ‘𐙓’. The assumption that 𐙢 and 𐙜 are equivalent in some way is critical here and rests heavily on the premise that the total of 25[.35] units accounted for in HT123b does related to the total of 25.35 units of 𐙜 yielded on the other side of the tablet. On the other hand this relation seems so strong that it can’t be coincidental, so on that basis it does force me to infer 𐙢 is somehow an equivalent yield product to 𐙜 or some quantifiably equivalent product for the purposes of allocation.

𐙢 is listed elsewhere but only so much as to reinforce its treatment as a logogram for a commodity. There is no clue provided by the instances elsewhere as to the nature of the commodity.
